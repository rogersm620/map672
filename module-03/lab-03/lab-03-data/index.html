Quarrel for the Capital

Cities that vied to become Kentucky's state capital at the turn of the 20th century.

This map includes the locations of the three cities that fought over who would become the home of the Kentucky state capital during the turn of the 20th century. In the latter half of the 1800's, the Kentucky Legislature determined that current capital building (built in 1827) no longer fit the needs of the state government. The legislature approved funds to begin construction of a new building in 1869, but construction was eventually halted until the new site could be agreed upon, with both Louisville and Lexington wanting to become the new capitol. Following the assasination of Governor William Goebel in 1899, outside the old capital building, the decsision was made to keep the state capital in Frankfort. Construction began in 1904, with the offical ground breaking ceremony taking place in August of 1905. The capital building is still in use today.
